# Solunatus Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Solunatus welcomes contributors from all backgrounds, skill levels, and points of view.  
We aim to maintain a friendly, collaborative environment where people feel comfortable sharing ideas and learning from each other.

Everyone participating in the project is expected to treat others with respect and kindness.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive and welcoming environment:

#### Communication
- **Using clear and welcoming language**: Write in a way that's accessible to people of all skill levels
- **Being patient and helpful**: Remember that everyone was a beginner once
- **Asking thoughtful questions**: Seek to understand before judging
- **Sharing knowledge generously**: Help others learn and grow
- **Acknowledging contributions**: Give credit where credit is due

#### Collaboration
- **Being respectful of differing opinions**: Consider alternative viewpoints with an open mind
- **Focusing on technical merit**: Evaluate ideas based on their technical value
- **Providing constructive feedback**: Offer specific, actionable suggestions
- **Accepting feedback gracefully**: View criticism as an opportunity to improve
- **Keeping discussions on-topic**: Stay focused on project goals

#### Community
- **Welcoming newcomers**: Help new contributors feel comfortable
- **Celebrating diversity**: Value different perspectives and experiences
- **Assuming good intentions**: Give people the benefit of the doubt
- **Leading by example**: Model the behavior you want to see
- **Supporting project goals**: Work together toward shared objectives

### Unacceptable Behaviors

The following behaviors are not acceptable in our community:

#### Harassment and Discrimination
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments
- Harassment based on race, ethnicity, nationality, gender identity, sexual orientation, religion, age, disability, or other protected characteristics
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Deliberately intimidating, stalking, or following
- Publishing others' private information (doxxing) without explicit consent

#### Disruptive Conduct
- Trolling or inflammatory comments intended to provoke
- Sustained disruption of discussions or project work
- Repeatedly derailing conversations from their intended topic
- Spamming or excessive self-promotion
- Bad faith arguments or intentional misinformation

#### Hostile Communication
- Excessively confrontational or aggressive language
- Threats of violence or violent language
- Discriminatory jokes or language
- Deliberately misgendering or using inappropriate names
- Persistent unwanted contact after requests to stop

### Handling Disagreements

Disagreements are a normal part of any collaborative project. When they arise:

1. **Focus on facts and technical reasoning**: Base arguments on evidence and logic
2. **Assume positive intent**: Believe others are trying to help the project
3. **Stay professional**: Keep emotions in check and communicate respectfully
4. **Seek common ground**: Look for areas of agreement
5. **Know when to step back**: Take a break if discussions become heated
6. **Escalate appropriately**: Contact maintainers if resolution isn't possible

Remember: **It's okay to disagree about technical approaches, but it's not okay to be disagreeable to people.**

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

#### For Project-Specific Issues
- Contact the repository owner or maintainers privately
- Open a confidential GitHub issue (if the platform supports it)
- Use GitHub's "Report abuse or spam" feature for serious violations

#### For Platform-Wide Violations
- Report directly to [GitHub Support](https://support.github.com/) under their Community Guidelines
- For immediate safety concerns, contact appropriate authorities

### What to Include in Reports

When reporting a violation, please provide:
- Your contact information (so we can follow up)
- Names/usernames of people involved
- Description of the incident
- Links to relevant conversations or content
- Any other context that would be helpful
- Whether you've attempted to resolve the issue directly

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality.

### Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these steps for handling violations:

#### 1. Warning
Most first-time minor violations will result in a private written warning, clarifying the nature of the violation and explaining why the behavior was inappropriate.

#### 2. Temporary Restriction
For repeated or more serious violations:
- Temporary ban from commenting or contributing
- Duration depends on severity (typically 1-30 days)
- Clear explanation of the restriction and conditions for return

#### 3. Permanent Ban
For severe or repeated violations after warnings:
- Permanent removal from all project spaces
- Public record of the ban (without details of the violation)
- No further communication with the project

### Our Commitment

We are committed to:
- **Fairness**: All participants are held to the same standards
- **Transparency**: Enforcement actions are documented and consistent
- **Privacy**: Reporter identities are protected
- **Timeliness**: Reports are addressed promptly
- **Good faith**: We trust participants to act in good faith and help maintain a thoughtful, constructive atmosphere

### Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was taken in error:
- You may appeal by contacting the maintainer privately
- Provide your reasoning and any relevant context
- Appeals will be reviewed within 7 days
- Decisions on appeals are final
